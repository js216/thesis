\begin{abstract}
   The \CENTREX\ experiment aims to provide a 30-fold increase in sensitivity
   over the best present upper bounds on the strength of time-violating
   fundamental interactions by measuring shifts in nuclear resonant frequencies
   in thallium fluoride. We describe the motivation for studying these
   time-violating interactions, and provide an overview of past work. We present
   the current measurement scheme for this experiment. Since the apparatus is
   still under construction, we present the components already built, with
   focus on the beam source and magnetic field control.
\end{abstract}
